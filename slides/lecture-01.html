What is economics?

The study of how people make choices under scarcity — and how those choices interact.

  • Resources are limited; wants are not.
  • Every choice has a cost.
  • Incentives shape behavior.

Scarcity

There is never enough of everything for everyone.

So we must choose — and choosing means giving something up.

Opportunity cost

The opportunity cost of a choice is the value of the next-best alternative you gave up.

An hour studying economics is an hour not spent sleeping, working, or with friends.

Thinking at the margin

Good decisions compare marginal benefit with marginal cost:

\[ \text{Do it if } \quad MB > MC \]

Not "all or nothing" — just one more unit.

Production possibilities

A country with limited resources faces trade-offs between any two goods — say, guns and butter.

  • Points on the frontier: efficient.
  • Inside: wasteful (idle resources).
  • Outside: not (yet) attainable.
