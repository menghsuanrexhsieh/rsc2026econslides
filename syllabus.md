## Course description

Economics is the study of how people make choices under scarcity, and how those
choices interact through markets and institutions. This course gives you the core
toolkit — supply and demand, marginal thinking, incentives, and trade-offs — and
then uses it to reason about real questions in growth, money, inequality, and
public policy.

## Learning objectives

By the end of the course you will be able to:

1. Explain how prices coordinate decisions in a market economy.
2. Use marginal analysis to model the choices of consumers and firms.
3. Distinguish microeconomic from macroeconomic questions and tools.
4. Read a basic economic model and interpret what it does and does not say.
5. Build and defend an evidence-based argument about an economic policy.

## Policies

**Attendance.** The course is short and cumulative — attend every session if you
can. Let the instructor know in advance if you must miss one.

**Collaboration.** Discuss problem sets freely, but write up your own answers in
your own words. The capstone is individual work.

**Accessibility.** If you need an accommodation to participate fully, contact the
instructor as early as possible and it will be arranged.

**A note on this syllabus.** Topics and dates may shift to match the pace of the
group. Any changes will be announced in class and reflected on this page.
